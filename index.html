About me:

I really like traveling, making new friends. I really wanted to participate in the Erasmus project because I saw it as an opportunity to make new friends and get to know cultures different from mine.

















ACTIVITIES

Debate

In our Erasmus project we had a debate about a film that we watched before travelling to Denmark. The film was interesting, while the debate was a bit difficult but in my opinion finally we all enjoyed the debate activity.

  • Blinded by the Light

    In “Blinded by the Light,” Javed, a Pakistani British teenager living in a London suburb called Luton in 1987, discovers the music of Bruce Springsteen thanks to his future friend Roops, and this music makes him reconsider all his future plans, to start thinking different about his writings and love himself as he is. The lyrics swirling around him, in his soul, he’s more than just a born-again Bruce fan. Bruce’s music becomes his obsession and identity, his runaway American dream that later came true after he won in a competition thanks to her teacher Ms Clay that generally inspired him to continue writing and believe in himself. He just about syncs his heartbeat to that sound and it gives him the courage to do things he wouldn’t have dared otherwise: like, for instance, asking Eliza , a girl in his literature class, out on a date and later even kiss her. In my opinion its a very good film to point out that all of us have the same rights to live in this world and nobody can take that away from us. At the same time the film shows that everything can come true, the only thing you need is to believe in yourself and fight for your dreams. Made by Pavel Izrailskiy
